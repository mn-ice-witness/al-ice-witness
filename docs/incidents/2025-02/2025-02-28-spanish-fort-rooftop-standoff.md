# Construction Worker Spends Six Hours on Rooftop to Avoid ICE Arrest

## Summary
In late February or early March 2025, a construction worker in Spanish Fort became viral after spending six hours on a building's roof to avoid arrest by federal immigration officials. The standoff drew significant media attention and became a symbol of the fear gripping immigrant workers in Baldwin County's construction industry. The worker eventually came down from the roof but declined to comment when approached by media.

## Community Impact
The incident highlighted the extreme measures workers felt compelled to take to avoid detention. It became a widely-shared example of the climate of fear in Alabama's construction industry and contributed to reports that 68% of construction projects statewide were experiencing delays due to worker shortages from raids and fear of enforcement.
