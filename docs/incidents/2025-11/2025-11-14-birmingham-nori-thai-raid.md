# 18 Arrested in ICE Raid at Nori Thai and Sushi Restaurant in Birmingham

## Summary
On November 14, 2025, ICE agents arrested 18 people at Nori Thai and Sushi in Birmingham's Altadena Square shopping center. The operation was described as an "arrest warrant operation centered around two aliens that had overstayed their visas." During the arrest of those two targets, 16 additional undocumented workers were encountered and arrested. The restaurant was not the primary target of the operation.

## Sources
1. WBRC (Nov 17, 2025): [Nori Thai and Sushi restaurant dining room closed after ICE raid](https://www.wbrc.com/2025/11/17/nori-thai-sushi-restaurant-dining-room-closed-after-ice-raid/)
2. ABC 33/40 (Nov 2025): [ICE arrests 18 at Birmingham's Nori Thai and Sushi in immigration raid](https://abc3340.com/news/local/ice-arrests-18-at-birminghams-nori-thai-and-sushi-in-immigration-raid)
3. WVTM13 (Nov 2025): [Alabama Birmingham Thai restaurant ICE immigration detain](https://www.wvtm13.com/article/alabama-birmingham-thai-restaurant-ice-immigration-detain/69479177)

## Affected Individual(s)
- **Primary targets:** 2 (visa overstays with arrest warrants)
- **Additional arrested:** 16 (encountered during operation)
- **Total arrested:** 18

## Business Impact
Following the raid:
- Dining room closed
- Restaurant operated takeout/delivery only
- Limited menu due to staffing losses

## Restaurant Statement
"We are heartbroken to share that our restaurant, Nori, was raided by ICE. We have lost many wonderful members of our team - people who have helped make Nori the place you know and love."

## Community Response
Customers rallied to support the restaurant, citing not just the food but the family atmosphere the staff had created. The incident highlighted how enforcement actions targeting specific individuals can devastate businesses when additional workers are detained.

## Editorial Assessment
**HIGH** - Extensively documented by WBRC, ABC 33/40, and WVTM13. Restaurant's public statement confirmed the impact. ICE confirmed the operation was targeting two specific individuals, with 16 additional arrests occurring during the operation.
