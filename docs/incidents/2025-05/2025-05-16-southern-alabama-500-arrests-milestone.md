# Immigration Arrests in Southern Alabama Reach 500+ in 3.5 Months

## Summary
By May 16, 2025, federal law enforcement officials announced they had reached 500+ immigration-related arrests in southern Alabama over approximately 3.5 months (since late January 2025). The arrests included 9 accused members of a Venezuelan gang. The Southern District of Alabama encompasses 12 counties, with enforcement concentrated in the Mobile-Baldwin corridor.

## Context
The 500+ arrest milestone represented a significant escalation in enforcement compared to previous years. The pace of approximately 143 arrests per month, or nearly 5 per day, reflected the prioritization of immigration enforcement in coastal Alabama. The construction industry, particularly in Baldwin County, was heavily impacted.
