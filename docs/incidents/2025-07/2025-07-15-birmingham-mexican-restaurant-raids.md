# 48 Detained in Coordinated ICE Raids on Mexican Restaurants Across Alabama

## Summary
On July 15, 2025, federal agents conducted simultaneous raids on eight Mexican restaurants across Alabama, detaining 48 people. While officials claimed the operation was part of a fraud investigation, only three people were placed in federal custody on criminal charges. Advocacy groups called the operation a "false flag" designed to meet deportation quotas, noting the massive resources deployed yielded minimal criminal evidence.

## Affected Individual(s)
- **Number detained by ICE:** 48
- **Federal custody (criminal charges):** 3
- **Primary target:** Cesar Campos-Reyes (charged with bank fraud, wire fraud, money laundering)

## Locations Raided
- El Patron Mexican Grill, Prattville
- El Jefe, Auburn (near Auburn-Opelika Mall)
- El Patron, Opelika (Tiger Town)
- Six additional restaurants across Alabama

## Agencies Involved
- Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE)
- FBI
- DEA
- U.S. Marshals
- Homeland Security Task Force (HSTF)
- Gulf of America Homeland Security Task Force

## Evidence Recovered
During searches at one location, agents recovered:
- Distribution quantities of methamphetamine
- Crack cocaine
- Powder cocaine
- Assorted pills
- More than $100,000 in cash
- At least 20 firearms

## Criminal Charges Filed
- **Cesar Campos-Reyes:** Bank fraud, wire fraud, money laundering (pleaded not guilty). Accused of fraudulently securing approximately $225,000 in PPP loans.
- **Two unnamed individuals:** Harboring illegal immigrants
- **One unnamed individual:** Drug trafficking

## Official Accounts

### Federal Position
Federal officials described the operation as a years-long investigation into business ownership and operations, focusing on PPP loan fraud and other financial crimes.

Birmingham Migra Watch, a project of the Alabama Coalition for Immigrant Justice, called the operation a "false flag":

## Community Impact
Immigration attorneys in Birmingham urged community members to know their rights following the raids. The disproportionate number of ICE detentions (48) compared to actual criminal arrests (3) raised questions about whether immigration enforcement was the primary goal rather than the stated fraud investigation.
