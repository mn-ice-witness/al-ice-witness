# 11 Detained in ICE Raid at Loxley Elementary School Construction Site

## Summary
On July 23, 2025, agents with the Gulf of America Homeland Security Task Force conducted an immigration raid at the construction site of the new Loxley Elementary School in Baldwin County, Alabama. Eleven workers were detained. Eyewitnesses described workers fleeing across fields and climbing into attics to evade agents who arrived on four-wheelers with a helicopter overhead. This was the second immigration raid at a public school construction site in south Alabama in less than a month.

## Affected Individual(s)
- **Number detained:** 11
- **Occupations:** Bricklayers, roofers, construction workers
- **Status:** FBI confirmed many workers on site were documented and were not arrested

## Timeline
- **Just before 9:00 a.m.** - Federal agents arrive at the Loxley Elementary School construction site on County Road 64
- **Raid begins** - Agents arrive on four-wheelers; helicopter circles overhead; gates are blocked off
- **Workers flee** - Workers run toward fields; four-wheelers chase them back toward the school building
- **Pursuit inside** - Some workers climb onto roofs and hide in attics; one agent falls through drywall pursuing suspects
- **30 minutes later** - Final worker apprehended after climbing to far side of building's roof
- **After raid** - 11 workers in total taken into custody; work resumes at the site

## Witness Accounts

### Irv Ballonoff (Construction Worker)
Ballonoff, who had been on-site for approximately three weeks, provided a detailed eyewitness account:

> "Gates were blocked off and a helicopter flew overhead."

On the pursuit:
> "They started running out to the fields but then the four wheelers came out into the fields, and they started running back to the school because that's the only place they can hide."

On workers hiding:
> "[Workers were] jumping in the roofs and climbing around in the attics."

Ballonoff noted that one agent fell through drywall while pursuing suspects, and one worker took approximately 30 additional minutes to apprehend after climbing to the far side of the building's roof.

## Official Accounts

### FBI Mobile
FBI Mobile confirmed 11 people were arrested. Many of the workers on site were documented workers and were not arrested.

### Baldwin County Public Schools
> "Baldwin County Public Schools is cooperating with federal investigators following a raid at the new Loxley Elementary School construction site. Our contracts with our construction projects require compliance with federal law. At this time, we have no indication of any wrongdoing."

## Context
This was the second immigration enforcement action at a public school construction site in south Alabama in less than a month. On June 24, 2025, 37 undocumented workers were arrested at the new Gulf Shores High School construction site.

Combined with the Gulf Shores raid, nearly 50 workers were arrested at the two Baldwin County school construction sites.

The Loxley Elementary School is scheduled to open by Fall 2026. Work resumed after the raid.
